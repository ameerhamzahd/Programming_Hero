Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

If your helmet is over 5 years old, has visible cracks, dents, frayed straps, or has been involved in an accident, it’s time to replace it. Helmets lose effectiveness over time due to wear and material degradation.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Before riding, check the tires for cracks or flat spots, inspect the brakes and gears, ensure the chain is clean and lubricated, and look for rust or damage. Test ride it to confirm everything works properly.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Always wear a helmet, follow traffic signals, ride in the bike lane when available, use hand signals, and stay visible with lights or reflective gear. Obey local traffic laws and stay alert to your surroundings.
